Global Engineering Logistics Intern

BOSCH Automotive Division | Madrid, Spain | January 2025 – June 2025

During my international internship at the BOSCH corporate office in Madrid, I supported daily engineering supply chain operations for over 80 high-value automotive sensors and 300 associated components. Working in a trilingual environment (Spanish, English, German), I managed and refined multi-level Bill of Materials (BOM) structures to ensure data integrity across the logistics chain. By optimizing procurement and material planning workflows using SAP and advanced Excel modeling, I significantly improved allocation accuracy and reduced lead-time variability. Additionally, I led data analysis on inventory levels and supplier KPIs, coordinating complex material movements between BOSCH plants and external suppliers across the European Union to maintain seamless production continuity.

CAMINOS Scholar & STEM Educator

NAU Center for STEM Teaching & Learning (NSF-Funded) | Colorado Plateau | Fall 2023 – Present

Serving as a research assistant for the National Science Foundation (NSF)-funded FEWSION for Community Resilience (F4R™) project, my work focuses on resource equity and science communication for Food, Energy, and Water (FEW) networks within Indigenous communities on the Colorado Plateau. I conduct preliminary field research planning utilizing X-ray Fluorescence (XRF) spectroscopy and drone technology for advanced mineral composition testing of agricultural land. My recent projects include modeling a mudflow dam to collect clean water for Monument Valley farms, and developing community-facing K-12 STEM curricula—such as the Garden Almanac and peach tree garden initiatives—to promote science literacy at local schools like the STAR School.
